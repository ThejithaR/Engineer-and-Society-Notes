# 09 · Quiz Concept Bank — CA 2 Prep 🎯

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Read this first.** Every past-batch quiz (Quiz 01, 20 Batch, 21 Batch — all versions) uses the **same 50-question bank**, just **reshuffled** (questions *and* option order change; the *concept* never does). If your CA 2 follows the pattern, mastering these 50 concepts ≈ full marks.

> [!TIP]
> **The one rule that answers ~90% of questions:** choose the option that favours
> **public safety → transparency → disclosure → honesty → fairness → sustainability → competence.**
> Reject anything about <span style="color:#c0392b">profit-first, secrecy, concealment, deadlines-over-safety, favouritism, bribes, or ignoring problems.</span>

## 🧩 Bucket 1 — Professional Ethics  ([Professional Ethics](<../02 · Professional Ethics/README.md>))

| # | Concept asked | ✅ Correct answer | ❌ Trap option |
|:-:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Accuracy & honesty of work | Ensure all info is **truthful & transparent** | Conceal mistakes; manipulate data |
| 2 | Purpose of a professional **code of ethics** | Ensure **welfare of clients & the public** | Legal protection; limit competition |
| 3 | Definition of **professional ethics** | **Guidelines established by professional organisations** | Personal values & morals of individuals |
| 4 | **NOT** a common ethical principle | **Profit maximisation** | (Objectivity, Integrity, Confidentiality *are*) |
| 5 | Priority in an **ethical dilemma** | **Welfare of society & the environment** | Employer interest; personal gain |
| 6 | Role of **whistleblowers** | **Report unethical behaviour to external authorities** | Cover up; ignore for job security |
| 7 | **Primary responsibility** of engineers | **Public safety, health & welfare** | Maximise employer profit; obey blindly |
| 8 | **Lifelong learning** | **Continuously update skills & knowledge** | Rely on past experience |
| 9 | **Diversity & inclusivity** | **Advocate equal opportunity & representation for all** | Hire the privileged; exclude groups |
| 10 | Ethical/responsible use of **technology** | Design tech that **respects human rights & promotes wellbeing** | Profit over ethics |
| 11 | Ethical use of **AI & robotics** | Design with **built-in safeguards & ethical considerations** | Ignore misuse concerns |
| 12 | **Environmental sustainability** | Design to **minimise negative environmental impacts** | Exploit resources; ignore environment |

## 🦺 Bucket 2 — Industrial Safety  ([Workplace Safety](<../03 · Workplace Safety/README.md>))

| # | Concept asked | ✅ Correct answer | ❌ Trap option |
|:-:|---|---|---|
| 13 | Role of **safety training** | Ensures employees **know procedures & protocols** | Only for certain roles; unnecessary |
| 14 | **Primary objective** of industrial safety | **Prevent accidents & injuries** | Maximise output; cut costs |
| 15 | A **proactive** safety measure | **Regular safety inspections & audits** | Treat injuries *after* accidents |
| 16 | **Hazard communication** programs | **Train employees to avoid hazards** | Conceal hazard info |
| 17 | Purpose of **PPE** | **Minimise risk of injury/illness** from hazards | Replace engineering controls; comfort |
| 18 | **Equipment maintenance** | **Minimise risk of equipment failure & accidents** | Increase output; cut costs |
| 19 | **NOT** a common hazard | **Excessive productivity** | (Chemical, ergonomic, noise *are*) |
| 20 | Why have **emergency response plans** | **Comply with regulatory requirements** ⚠️ | "Reduce likelihood of accidents" (**marked wrong!**) |
| 21 | Purpose of **safety drills** | Ensure staff **familiar with emergency procedures** | Disruption; entertainment |
| 22 | Promote a **safety culture** | **Lead by example & encourage safe practices** | Incentivise shortcuts; punish reporters |
| 23 | **NOT** a safety responsibility | **Prioritising deadlines over employee safety** | (Training, reporting, compliance *are*) |
| 24 | Objective of **OHS regulations** (labour law) | **Minimise workplace accidents & injuries** | Lower costs; reduce productivity |

> [!WARNING]
> **Q20 is the single most-missed question** (both reviewed scripts got it wrong). For *"why have emergency response plans?"* the answer key says **"to comply with regulatory requirements"** — **NOT** "to reduce the likelihood of accidents." Don't overthink it; pick compliance.

## 🤝 Bucket 3 — Conflict of Interest  ([Conflict of Interest](<../04 · Conflict of Interest/README.md>))

| # | Concept asked | ✅ Correct answer | ❌ Trap option |
|:-:|---|---|---|
| 25 | Importance of **addressing COI** | Ensures **fairness, integrity & trust** | COI is unavoidable; ignore it |
| 26 | Avoid COI on **committees/boards** | **Disclose** & **abstain** from relevant decisions | Promote personal interest |
| 27 | **Address** COI in practice | **Disclose** & seek guidance from relevant parties | Ignore to avoid complications |
| 28 | Action that is **NOT** a COI | **Reporting unsafe conditions** to authorities | Accepting commission; investing in supplier |
| 29 | COI **policies** & credibility | **Guidelines to manage conflicts transparently** | Limit independent decisions |
| 30 | Scenario most likely a **COI** | **Accepting a gift from a client** | Reporting a violation; disclosing |
| 31 | Correct course of action on a COI | **Disclose** & seek guidance (supervisor/ethics committee) | Hide it; alter records |
| 32 | Purpose of **COI policies** in firms | **Maintain transparency & integrity** in decisions | Prioritise profit over ethics |
| 33 | **Proactive** COI mitigation | **Disclose personal relationships** that could influence | Conceal financial interests |
| 34 | Best **definition** of COI | Engineers **prioritise personal gain over responsibilities** | Disagreeing on specs; fair pay |
| 35 | **Manage** COI during execution | **Foster an open, transparent work culture** | Encourage secrecy |
| 36 | **Ethical obligation** re COI | **Always disclose** potential conflicts | Overlook to meet timelines |

> [!TIP]
> If you see **"disclose"** (often with **"abstain/recuse"** or **"seek guidance"**) — it's almost certainly correct. **Disclosure = Identify → Disclose → Manage.**

## 🧠 High-frequency "NOT / EXCEPT" questions (easy marks)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> These flip the logic — they ask for the **odd one out**. Memorise the odd answer:
> | Question | The odd-one-out answer |
> |---|---|
> | NOT a common **hazard** | **Excessive productivity** |
> | NOT a common **ethical principle** | **Profit maximisation** |
> | NOT a key **procurement principle** | **Confidentiality** |
> | NOT a **safety responsibility** | **Prioritising deadlines over safety** |
> | Action that is **NOT a COI** | **Reporting unsafe conditions to authorities** |

## ⏱️ 60-second pre-exam checklist

> [!TIP]
> - [ ] **Public safety & welfare** is *always* the engineer's #1 priority.
> - [ ] On COI → **Disclose + recuse/abstain**.
> - [ ] On procurement → **transparency, competition, value for money**; **confidentiality is NOT** a principle.
> - [ ] On safety → **prevent** (proactive); PPE = last line; **emergency plans = regulatory compliance**.
> - [ ] **Profit maximisation** is never an ethical principle; **bribes/kickbacks** are never acceptable.
> - [ ] Labour: **collective = groups**; **at-will = employer can fire anytime**; **min wage = living wage**; **anti-discrimination = protected characteristics**.
> - [ ] Whistleblowing, lifelong learning, diversity, sustainability, AI safeguards → all **good** (pick them).
